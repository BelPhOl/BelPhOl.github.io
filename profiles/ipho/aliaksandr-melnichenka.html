Research

1) Faraday-screen diagnostics for magnetized turbulence — First author

Mentors: Alex Lazarian (UW–Madison), Dmitri Pogosyan (U. Alberta)

Problem. Recover 3D magnetic turbulence statistics from single-frequency polarization (Faraday-thin regime).

Approach. Derived a compact two-point angular diagnostic; validated on 512³ synthetic MHD cubes; benchmarked against archival RM grids.

Result. Robust scaling consistent with Kolmogorov-type cascades across seeds and broken spectra (3/2, −5/3).

Artifacts. ApJ submission (2025) · repo @ tag v0.3, `run.sh` reproduces Fig.2 · talk slides when available

My role. Theory derivations, simulations, figures, manuscript lead.

2024-2025

2) Self-Interacting Dark Matter: drift & diffusion via Monte-Carlo/Langevin — Lead developer

Mentors: MIT Kavli (Mark Vogelsberger; with Xuejian (Jacob) Shen; Vinh Tran)

Problem. Compute orbit-averaged drift/diffusion coefficients for velocity-dependent σ(v).

Approach. Local MC/Langevin solver; acceptance–rejection; convergence tests; recovery of constant-σ limits.

Result. Validated coefficients across regimes; packaged solver for reuse.

Artifacts. sidm-drift · Docs/Examples · arXiv methods note (in prep)

My role. Algorithm design, implementation, tests, figures.

2024-2025

3) Kapitsa-like instabilities in electron fluids — Contributor (simulation)

Mentors: Leonid Levitov (MIT)

Problem. Hydrodynamic roll-wave instabilities in electronic bands.

Approach. Python simulations + dispersion-relation checks; cross-validation with Mathematica implementation.

Result. Working regime map; short note in preparation.

Artifacts. figures · notes · sim code

My role. Simulation implementation and validation.
